Why did you become a nurse?

I always wanted to be in the medical/health field growing up. I thought about becoming a medical doctor when I was in college. I enjoyed the fact that nurses spend much more quality time with the patients. Therefore, I went for nursing.

What skills and personality traits do you think are important for nurses to possess?

Patience, attention to details, and the willingness to learn make a great nurse

Could you describe the difficult situation that you encounter as a nurse and what you've learned from it?

Covid has taught me a lot about how to be patient, compassionate, and to really put your patients before my own safety. I think being a nurse is a noble profession. Not a lot of people can say that their jobs are noble jobs and they are proud to be in their profession the same way I feel towards nursing.

Why do you want to work in the ICU as a nurse?

I am meticulous. I enjoy learning the details. ICU patients are sick. Most of them are in multiorgan failure. They need a holistic approach to effectively and safely care for them.

What are the challenges you have to face?

Being in the critical care setting is extremely rewarding. It is especially true if you want to make a big impact on someone's life when their lives are hanging by a thread. It could be extremely stressful. I know a few ICU nurses who were so burned out that they decided to choose another unit or quit nursing altogether after covid.

Do you have any goals and how do you work to achieve them?

My goal is to continue to deliver excellent care to my patients and to touch each of their lives with compassionate support.
